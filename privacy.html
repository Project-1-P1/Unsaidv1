UNSAID (“the Service,” “we,” “us,” or “Platform”) is an experimental, anonymous communication infrastructure. This document outlines the technical and legal realities of using the Service. Our architecture is designed to minimize data retention ("Privacy by Design").

1. Zero-Knowledge Architecture

UNSAID is architected to operate without knowing who you are. We do not possess the technical capability to link a user to their real-world identity.

1.1 No Personal Data Collection

We strictly do not collect, process, or store the following data categories:

  • Names, addresses, emails, or phone numbers.
  • Passwords, biometric data, or social media identifiers.
  • GPS location data or precise geolocation.
  • Device Identifiers (IDFA, AAID) or MAC addresses.

1.2 The "Session Alias" Mechanism

Identity on UNSAID is ephemeral. Upon accessing the Service, your browser locally generates a random cryptographic string (e.g., "Neon-Ghost-9921").

Technical Reality: This alias exists only in your browser's volatile memory (Session Storage). We do not maintain a master key or database linking this alias to an IP address. Once you close your browser tab, the key to that identity is destroyed on the client side, making re-identification technically impossible.

2. Data Lifecycle & Irreversible Deletion

2.1 Ephemeral Content

All User-Generated Content (UGC) is subject to a strict Time-to-Live (TTL) parameter chosen by the user (24 hours, 3 days, or 7 days).

2.2 The Purge Protocol

When the TTL expires, the following sequence occurs automatically:

  1. Soft Logic: Row Level Security (RLS) policies immediately block the content from being read via the API.
  2. Hard Deletion: Database cron jobs trigger a physical deletion of the row.
Defense of Non-Production: We cannot produce data that has been deleted. We do not maintain "shadow backups" or "cold storage" of expired messages for surveillance purposes. If a message is gone from the UI, it is gone from our infrastructure.

3. Third-Party Infrastructure

UNSAID utilizes Supabase (a Backend-as-a-Service provider) to host its database and realtime capabilities.

  • Data Processing: Supabase processes connection requests to facilitate data transfer. While standard TCP/IP logs may transiently exist at the network layer for DDoS protection, UNSAID does not ingest these logs for user profiling.

3.1 No Analytics or Ad Tech

We strictly do NOT use:

  • Behavioral analytics (e.g., Google Analytics, Mixpanel).
  • Targeted advertising pixels (e.g., Facebook Pixel).
  • Third-party tracking scripts or fingerprinting tools.
  • User profiling algorithms.

4. Community Moderation & Reporting

UNSAID relies on a community-driven moderation model to maintain safety without compromising anonymity.

  • Anonymous Reporting: Users may report content for spam or abuse. These reports do not identify the reporter.
  • Safety Protocol: Reports are used solely for content moderation. Content removal does not imply identification, attribution, or investigation of any user.
  • No Logs: The act of reporting does not create a persistent user record.

5. Children's Privacy

UNSAID is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction).

Compliance Statement: We do not knowingly collect or process data from children. If we become aware that a child has used the Service, we will take immediate steps to delete any associated information. If you believe a child has used the Service, please discontinue use immediately.

7. Law Enforcement & Subpoenas

We cooperate with law enforcement to the extent legally required, but we are constrained by our architecture.

"Cannot Give What We Do Not Have": If served with a valid subpoena or court order, we can only provide data that actually exists in the database at that exact moment. We cannot provide:
  • The real name or email of a user (We do not collect it).
  • Content that has already expired (It is deleted).
  • Future decryption keys for user sessions (We do not possess them).

8. Global & Regional Compliance

8.1 GDPR (Europe) & CCPA (California)

Since we do not collect "Personal Data" linked to an identified or identifiable natural person, the majority of processing requirements do not apply. However, we grant rights to Erasure simply by allowing messages to expire.

For users in India, UNSAID operates as an "Intermediary" as defined under Section 2(1)(w) of the Information Technology Act, 2000.

Safe Harbor Protection (Section 79): Under Section 79 of the IT Act, 2000, UNSAID acts solely as a technical facilitator. We are not liable for any third-party information, data, or communication link made available or hosted by us.

Grievance Mechanism: In compliance with the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021, if you encounter content that violates Indian law, you must report it to our Grievance Officer via the contact email below. Upon receiving actual knowledge of unlawful content via a court order, we will remove such content within 36 hours.

DPDP Act, 2023 Compliance: As UNSAID does not collect "Personal Data" (data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data), the processing obligations of a Data Fiduciary do not apply to our session-based architecture.
